# CD recommendations & track listings

For maximum acceptability of subject matter, I’ve kept this list to abstract instrumental music, and for the 5 releases I list that are *mostly* abstract apart from one or two tracks, I’ve warned about those tracks in case anyone wants to omit them. But for the sake of brevity I won’t explicitly note that an instrumental piece is meant to be “about the countryside” etc if that’s obvious from its title.

In our home, most CDs listed here are on our living-room rack and also available from our Alexa devices (served legally via our Raspberry Pi 1). Alexa commands given here will not work outside our home, and even within it will work only if heard correctly (otherwise you’ll likely get a [random Spotify track](spotify.md) instead).

Beethoven symphonies 1-8.

Ideally you should use a historically informed (“period”) performance such as Gardiner’s (which also has a talk on why they didn’t modernise it). Anyone who wants strictly instrumental music may wish to stop with number 8 (or the 3<sup>rd</sup> movement of number 9), since the 4<sup>th</sup> movement of number 9 ends with a choral ode whose lyrics might offend some (it’s a modified German poem that blends imagery from Greek and Christian religions and alludes to drunkenness) although it does include some good lines. The Hanover Band boxed set also includes the *Missa Solemnis* (which you might want to omit if you wish to stay clear of the church tradition of the Mass) and Overtures (which are instrumental but were written for use with stories you may or may not like), but it’s easy to leave these out if you want to hear only symphonies. The Hanover Band recordings happened to be made in churches, but the symphonies are *not* church music.
